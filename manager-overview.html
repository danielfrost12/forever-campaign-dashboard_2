Analysis

At 1,618 followers against 15.3K monthly listeners, the follow-to-listen ratio is ~10.5%. For independent electronic artists, this is in the average range — typical ratios span 5–15%. The ratio against total audience (355K) is much lower at 0.45%, but this is expected given that 71% of that audience is "programmed" (algorithm-served listeners who may have heard one track in a playlist without ever visiting the profile).

Opportunity Gap

This is the most actionable gap in the data. With 355K people who've heard Alexander's music, converting even 2–3% into followers would 3–5x the follower count. Tactics: Spotify follow-gated playlists, pre-save campaigns, and "follow for early access" prompts in ad creative. This needle can be moved quickly.

What this means: Followers are the one metric that's "average" rather than "above average." This is actually good news — it means there's a clear, actionable growth lever that can be pulled immediately. The audience exists; the conversion just hasn't been optimized yet.
